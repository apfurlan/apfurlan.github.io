I studied physics at Universidade Federal do Paraná (Curitiba, Brazil), where I developed a strong foundation in theoretical and computational methods. I earned my M.Sc. and Ph.D. from Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul (Porto Alegre, Brazil), with a research period at the Institute of Physical Chemistry Rocasolano (Madrid, Spain). My doctoral and postdoctoral work focused on statistical mechanics, phase transitions, and complex systems. From 2017 to 2021, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais, collaborating with Prof. Ronald Dickman.

Building on my physics background, I transitioned into data engineering and cloud infrastructure. My career evolved from developing Power BI dashboards and ETL pipelines to designing and automating cloud environments on AWS and Azure using Terraform, Airflow, and Kubernetes. Currently, I work at Cielo as Data Platform Specialist – Data, MLOps and LLMOps, applying the same analytical rigor from academic research to build scalable and automated data solutions.
